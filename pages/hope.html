HOPE

Survival / Exploration

Godot

Duration:

2 days

6

Platform:

PC

Context

This project was developed during a hackathon in collaboration with the Red Cross, with the goal of raising awareness about climate change and its potential impacts. It envisions the world as it could be in the year 2050 if current environmental trends continue.

Ambition

The vision behind the game was to immerse the player in a realistic and tragic scenario set in the year 2050, where environmental collapse has made survival a daily struggle. The story follows a young migrant girl navigating a world heavily polluted and suffering from extreme food scarcity.

Throughout the game, players can explore, interact with objects, structures, and people, and make choices that shape their experience. Our core ambition was to highlight how, even in the most desperate situations, human connection, mutual aid, and empathy remain the most powerful tools for survival.

We aimed to create a balance between the harsh, dramatic reality of a world deeply damaged by human actions, and the resilient, compassionate responses of people facing crisis together.

My Work

As a Game Designer, I collaborated closely with the team to define the core gameplay experience. Together, we designed the foundational elements of the game, including the 3Cs, key features, base dialogues, and the level design of the main street area.

On the programming side, I implemented key gameplay systems, including:

  • The player movement system with a state machine handling idle, walking, and interacting states
  • Camera behavior, including zoom in/out transitions during interactions
  • Lootable object animations for enhanced feedback and immersion
  • A custom Input Manager to handle player controls across different contexts
  • A Sound Manager to manage in-game audio, including SFX and ambient sounds
